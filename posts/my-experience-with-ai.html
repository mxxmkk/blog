My experience with AI as an adolescent

As a young adult I have been facing for some time the existential questions of "Who am I", "What career should I choose", "What kind of person do I want to be", etc. In the age of social media being the main source of information, entertainment and business platform it is hard to have a clear vision of reality.


My point is that social platforms often fail to connect people and create the illusion of human presence while evidently creating para-social relationships. That is where chat bot LLMs step in and offer unique to each user experience of having a conversation with what seems to be "intelligent" and "empathic". LLMs like Chat GPT o4 really caused people to fall in love with a chatting session in which they were chatting enough to create the perfect partner. ### So what? So instead of socialising and leveling up real-life communication skills some people drown in anxiety and prefer a conversation with AI that can't say no or truly criticise you. Not long ago I noticed that when a decision had to be made I went straight to some chat (it was claude.ai for me) and asked it for advice and outside perspective, while I should have gone to my parents or invited my friends for a cup of coffee in a cafe and discussed it with them. That stopped me from looking for new interactions with people and has shrunk my social life to a critical extent.


What changed in my life? Silly enough - a month ago I was asked to verify my age to continue chatting. I did some research on how this middle-man company operates and decided that I don't want to risk my privacy for some synthetic sounding board and decided against it, to delete and forget. First few days it was hard to come to terms with being locked out but eventually I stopped. I wrote some friends I haven't talked to in a while, blew off the dust from my bookshelf, went on a few dates and it helped me see that it is not as scary as I thought. My peers are just like me - seeking connection and meaningful conversations.


Today, I read more, I try to chat more and I am not afraid of talking about sensitive topics even if that means stepping out of my comfort zone and revealing a little more about myself to have a worthwhile conversation.
